<p>So, I forgot I signed up to receive a free Beacon from Google a while ago &#8211; but it has just turned up this week at my work, here is a run down of what it does and how to receive one.</p>
<p>A beacon for those who don&#8217;t know is a small Bluetooth 4.0 Low Energy (BLE) device which can ping/make contact with mobile devices.</p>
<p>There are many 3rd party beacons on the market, however the free beacon I received was part of Google&#8217;s Project Beacon team. As of this post&#8217;s publish date; you can still sign-up for one here: <a href="https://services.google.com/fb/forms/projectbeaconbygoogle/">https://services.google.com/fb/forms/projectbeaconbygoogle/</a>. There is also more information on this page to learn more: <a href="https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/7577917">https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/7577917</a>.</p>
<p>Our beacon came already setup and linked to my employee&#8217;s Google MyBusiness account. The setup was simple, just enter the activation code onto a webpage and stick the beacon somewhere there is plenty of foot traffic, then just wait 2 to 3 weeks for the data to be collected and analysed.</p>
<p>Essentially these tasks can be accomplished with the free beacon we received:</p>
<ul>
<li>Higher visibility on a Google user&#8217;s Google maps account &#8211; only when people opt-in for location history</li>
<li>Allowing user-generated content (like photos and reviews) to be gathered from visitors to your venue</li>
<li>An increase the accuracy of &#8216;popular times&#8217; and visit durations to your venue</li>
<li>Insights into how user&#8217;s interact with your venue</li>
</ul>
<p>Finally, if you&#8217;re unsure if you should sign-up, or if you have already received one of these free beacons and are reading this article; I would say you should definitely do it and install it, there really are no-downsides I can think of.</p>
