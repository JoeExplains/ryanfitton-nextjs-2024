Podcasts can be thought of as shows, either video or radio shows. They cover certain topics you may be interested in. For me, I subscribe to a variety of Web Design and Technology related podcasts.

There are thousands to choose from, using websites such as [poodle.fm](http://poodle.fm/) and the [Yahoo Podcast directory](http://dir.yahoo.com/news_and_media/internet_broadcasts/podcasting_and_audioblogging/directories/)  will help you discover new podcasts. Apple's iTunes program also has a large variety of different podcast shows, you can learn more information about this on [this page](http://www.apple.com/uk/itunes/podcasts/) by Apple.

Below is a list of the podcasts I subscribe to:

-   The Linux Action Show -- [Website](http://www.jupiterbroadcasting.com/show/linuxactionshow/)
-   TechSnap -- [Website](http://www.jupiterbroadcasting.com/show/techsnap/)
-   Tech News Today -- [Website](http://twit.tv/show/tech-news-today/)
-   This Week In Tech (TWiT) -- [Website](http://twit.tv/show/this-week-in-tech/)
-   Mac Break Weekly -- [Website](http://twit.tv/show/macbreak-weekly/)
-   The Giz Wiz -- [Website](http://twit.tv/show/weekly-daily-giz-wiz/)
-   The Big Web Show -- [Website](http://5by5.tv/bigwebshow)
-   The Web Ahead -- [Website](http://5by5.tv/webahead)
-   Boagworld Podcast -- [Website](http://boagworld.com/show/)
